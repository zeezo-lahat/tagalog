pa-in:
Pinaluto ni Mario si Aling Tess ng pansit para sa handaan.
Mario had Aling Tess cook pansit for the celebration.

ipa-:
Ipapaluto ni Mario kay Aling Tess ang pansit para sa handaan.
Mario will have Aling Tess cook the pansit for the celebration.

Could also be:
Ipapaluto ni Mario ang pansit kay Aling Tess para sa handaan.

Could NOT be!!:
(WRONG!!) Ipapaluto ang pansit ni Mario kay Aling Tess para sa handaan. (WRONG!!)


ipag-in - causative + benefactive:
Ipinagluto ko ng sopas ang kapatid ko (dahil maysakit siya).
I cooked soup for my sister because she is sick.
The important thing here is the "maysakit siya".  The use of "ipag" here implies
that this is not an ordinary/routine thing and that there is a particular
reason for this happening.  If you do not state the reason, your listener will
be wondering about the reason.

compare:
   mahiyâ    to feel shy or timid
   mapahiyâ  to be shamed or humiliated

   mahirap  poor; hard to do
   pahiráp  becoming poor; becoming hard to do (also note stress shift)
   pahirap  burden, punishment

puwede, maari: "as with other modal verbs, they are used with the infinitive of
any given verb according to any focus"
