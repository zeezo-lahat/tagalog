Tagalog Reference Grammar, p.419
man does not occur in basic sentences.

it is mainly used with hindi or wala together with lamang/lang:

Hindi ka man lamang nakapag-almusal.	You didn't even get to eat breakfast.
Wala man lang siya roon.	She wasn't even there.

or in consecutive clauses, eg 'even if', 'even though':

(Kahit) mahal man ang sapatos, binili ko rin.	Even though the shoes were expensive, I still bought them.
Hindi ka rin maniniwala, (kahit) sabihin ko man ang totoo.	You won't believe it, even if I tell you the truth.

or in indefinite constructions ('whatever', 'whoever', etc.):

(Kahit) ano man ang gusto mo, ibibili kita.	Whatever you want, I'll buy it for you.
Sabihin mo kanino (or "sa kahit sino") pa mang dumating na umuwi na ako.	Tell whoever else comes that I've gone home.
(Kahit) saan man ako pumunta, nakikita ko siya.	Wherever I go, I see him.

in inverted (ie, topic-predicate "ay") sentence having din/rin in the predicate:

Ako man ay natakot din.	I was scared too (in fact).

Ang kalabaw man, napapagod din.	(The) carabaos too get tired (in fact.)

Kalabaw man, napapagod din.	Carabaos too get tired (in fact.)

Kung gabi man, mainit din.	Even at night, it's still hot.

where "o" ("or) joins two structures, meaning "either ... or":

Ikaw man o ako ay maaaring gumawa niyon.	Either you or I can do that.
Sa loob man o sa labas ay maaari tayong magsayawan.	We can hold the dance indoors or outdoors.
