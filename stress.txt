I think this might be a very special case where a root that starts with "pa" and also has a
"mag" and "magpa" needs a stress shift to differentiate them
Akó ang nagpapátay sa kaniyá.
"I am the one who ordered him killed."

Last example is different from [note the stress]:
Akó ang nagpapatáy sa kaniyá.
"I am the one who is killing him." (conjugate nagCV-, not conjugate nagpa-)

ROOTS

if a root has both noun and adj versions:
 - stress will normally be on the penultimate syllable for the noun root and the final syllable for adj version
 - the noun root is generally the one to use in order to determine the new shift

in the case of roots with penult stress:
 - SHIFT means to the (new) penultimate syllable
 - NO SHIFT means stress shifts to the new, final "ín" or "án"

in the case of roots with final stress:
 - SHIFT means stress shifts to the new, final "ín" or "án"
 - NO SHIFT means to the (new) penultimate syllable
