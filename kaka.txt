TRG p.423
lamang or lang is obligatory after a recent-perfective verb

Kakakain ko lamang.	I just ate.

TRG p.372
these correspond to actor focus verbs, and not all such verbs have this form.

um, ma, maka -> kaCV
mag -> kapapag or kapagCV
magbasa (read intensively) -> kapapagbasa or kapagbabasa (has just read intensively)
magbili (sell) -> kapapagbili or kapagbibili (has just sold)
magpagupit -> ka pa gu gupit, ka pa pa gupit or ka pag pa pa gupit (just had someone cut)
magpasulat -> ka pa su sulat, ka pa pa sulat or ka pag pa pa sulat (just had someone write)
manganak (give birth) -> ka pa pang anak or ka pangang anak

Kakain ko (pa) lang ng karne.	I just ate the/some meat.
Kakain ko (pa) lang sa karne.	I just ate the meat. (use "sa" to make the object definite.*)
Kapapasulat (pa) lang ng tatay kay Jose ng liham sa diyaryo	Dad just had Jose writ the/a letter to the newspaper.
Kapapanguha (pa) lang ng bata ng mga mangga.	The child has just gathered the/some mangoes.

for intensively recent perfective verbs, take the above forms and redup the root basically

* note: in other cases, "sa" object complements may occur only after nominalized verb forms.
